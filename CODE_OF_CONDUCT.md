# Code of Conduct

This project is a scholarly and community-oriented resource list. Contributors are expected to collaborate respectfully, carefully, and in good faith.

## Expected Conduct

- Be respectful toward other contributors, maintainers, researchers, educators, practitioners, and community members.
- Make evidence-based contributions and distinguish verified public information from uncertain leads.
- Use concise, neutral language when describing benchmarks, datasets, papers, and software.
- Respect benchmark licenses, dataset terms, citation expectations, and community norms.
- Be careful when discussing high-stakes domains such as medicine, law, autonomous driving, robotics, education, and public-sector decision making.
- Raise uncertainty through issues or pull-request discussion rather than presenting unclear resources as verified.

## Unacceptable Conduct

- Harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, intimidation, or sustained disruption.
- Adding copied benchmark data, restricted files, personal data, or unlicensed materials.
- Presenting unverified resources as open, reusable, or maintained.
- Misrepresenting benchmark scope, access conditions, licensing, or evaluation claims.
- Using the repository to promote unrelated tools, papers, services, or projects.

## Reporting and Response

If you see a problem, please open an issue or contact the repository maintainer. Maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions that violate this code, expose restricted material, or create legal, ethical, or scholarly risk.

The goal is to keep this list useful, trustworthy, and welcoming for the neuro-symbolic AI benchmarking community.
